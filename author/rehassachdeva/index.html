The phenomenal parables of “Pennelope the Penguin”

It all started when a wandering diary page was found. Impressed by its ingenious content, the owner was summoned to serve as the mascot of the Editorial board of Spring Dale Senior School, Amritsar. With a swanky pencil clenched in one hand, and a mammoth notebook in the other, swish glasses upon her pug nose, and a flamboyant greatness in her style, presenting… Pennelope-the Penguin!

Erudite and scholarly, agreed. But Pennelope (later famed as Penny), had a nasty habit of snatching food from unsuspecting students and gulping down gallons of coffee.

However her diligence and sense of commitment to board meetings, deadlines for magazine and newsletter tasks, was indeed creditable. Even the caterwauling weather was no deterrent to her eternal display of talent.

Then, all was quiet for some time, until… the bird with the gift of a natural tuxedo, had struck gold! She was offered a leading role in a Hollywood film titled “Chronicles of the Penguin Writer”

Eventually, she started missing the joys of life here at Spring Dales, and upon her return made concerted efforts to connect with old friends and make new ones. She struck upon the idea of putting up a picture of a small fish getting chased by a menacing big fish and asked people to scribble in what they thought was going on in the minds of the two fishes. The entries varied from the cute to the bizarre. Sample these…

1.  Big Fish: Why is my mouth open?                              Small Fish: Because some stupid students (childishly) have painted you that way.

2.  Big Fish: Dude, something stuck in my throat, please remove it…

Small fish: It’ll be my skeleton in there next, run for your life!

Another time, she raised the question. “Why are school buses always yellow?” As before, she received some incredibly interesting responses:

  1. Well, yellow=casein…casein found in milk… milk is good for children… milk comes from cows! Too bad they are not yellow but brown! 
  2. Sorry, but my school bus is white.
  3. Yellow is the colour of acceptance. That means whether you want to go to school or not, you’ll have to go.

Winter chill was receding; spring was on her way, and Penny being a restless sort, found it difficult to remain anchored to one spot for long. When she met a flock of migratory birds on their stopover, en route to the Siberian hinterland, the foolish creature felt the desire to answer the “call of the wild”. So she packed her bags and set sail for the Arctic.

Anyway, I’ve gone on long enough about her, and It’s time I introduced myself. I’m Mr. Bean, the snake, Penny’s neighbour and long time friend. Now that she’s gone (for a while atleast, ), I’ll be taking care of the stuff she was handling. I hope to run into you sometime. Till then, farewell!

How I spent my summer vacation

Titled – “Fleet of Time”

There are moments in life,

When time glides like little birds through the infinite sky.

When the thread you’re holding, unwinds its web, in the fleet of time.

The way smoke diffuses itself through the air we breathe.

June the tenth, you officially know, there’s no paranoia for atleast a month and a half.

The next thing you know, you’ve sailed away with the wind.

You refuse to believe. But, before you realize, the time’s gone.

You sleep late, you wake up late, and then comes the dreaded dawn.

July the twenty fifth, you blink your eyes hard, good morn!

You scratch your head, thinking hard as you can.

What did you even do, that time served you so bad, and away it ran.

You didn’t pick up the remote once! You deactivated your facebook!

You didn’t loiter around at parties and hill stations.

You didn’t spend nights in reading fiction books!

How did the time fly?! My home work’s undone! And is there an exam?!

Good heavens! You think what to do now?! And then, Bam!

Your mind boggles up with the rarest excuses.

And then, you’re late, dear take a look at the clock, at you, it bemuses.

You prepare yourself, for a day filled with cold reproaches.

You’re more nervous, with every second, as ‘9:35” approaches.

And when your teacher asks, what did you even do?

As honest and truthful as I can be, I tell her, what I’ve been up to.

That there’s no real vacation for a science student.

That every day and every night,

Your textbooks are your pals and pies.

And that is precisely why, there’s little to talk about it.

And that’s exactly why, this write-up is so plain upright.

It’s so hot!

The summer is here in its full grip and glory, all set with the singeing sun on its right wing and dusty, humid storms on its left, this time buckled down, more than ever, to crown its bloodthirsty army, by roasting and dehydrating each one of us. The battle has begun!

As we become lazier with each ticking degree that turns the air into a blazing-hot oven, here are some do’s and don’ts that we can observe to beat the heat.

Do’s:

Take fresh foods; drink plenty of water, fresh lime drinks, and fresh fruit juices. As for my medical friends, Cucumis melo and mangifera indica, help cool you down, after all the running around, don’t they? Make a point to make watermelons your best friends too. Not only do they quench you thirst, but are rich in fiber, vitamins A and C, potassium and sodium. I’m convinced they’re your favourite fruits, uh sorry-‘vegetables’, (yeah I didn’t know that too). They belong to the same family as cucumber, pumpkins etc.

What else? I don’t need to remind you of sunglasses and sunscreens. We are aware enough, aren’t we? But here are some don’ts:

Do not eat cut fruits or uncovered foods from outside. This is how you can protect yourselves from food and water borne infections. And I know, this sounds hard, but avoid fried foods and chocolates.

Eat ice-creams instead! Ha!

For all my friends who cannot help staying indoors at all, here are some tips to channelise you energies. Swimming and yoga are the best exercises that not only help you stay fit, but also soothe your nerves and enhance their endurance. No matter how hard the summer monster tries, they stay perfectly cool.

Okay, let’s not blame the summer season too much. It’s just a part of nature. Of course, it has its benefits. Did you know, just by going out into the park, focusing your eyes on something green, beneath the sun’s morning light, can eliminate your fatigue and balance your energy? So, forego your shoes, take a moment, feel good, and sanguinely thank Lord Surya, for keeping our bones healthy, and for being the ultimate source of energy. Settle a peace treaty with summer’s army by employing the above do’s and don’t’s, and then, it’ll not be so hot!

Humanity

Humanity has made little real progress over the past century or so. Technological innovations have taken place, but the overall condition of humanity is no better. War, violence, and poverty are still with us. Technology cannot change the condition of humanity.

In the cascading tides of the past century, amidst the seas of undreamed inventions, from probing beyond the light years of galaxies, to dividing endlessly into quarks and leptons, humanity has come a long way, much because of the tremendous multitudes of sparkling technologies which seem to have exceeded humanity itself.

Today Science manifests into its ultimate brilliance, changing the face of the world, and digitalizing all that it can ‘think of’. For instance, it has enabled us to connect eye-twinklingly around the globe; modernized production, and caused breakthroughs in health and medicine which have greatly added vitality to life and improved life expectancy rate. And though, one cannot run out of additions to this list, but we can certainly talk of it darker side equally well.

The same world that brims up with newer ideas and better ways to assist humanity, also continues to wretch the weak, and succor the strong. It is this very world where men become more materialistic with each passing day, where the unheard, and unfed grow into an appalling majority. Yes, I’m talking of those millions of children who can’t go to school because they can hardly dream of anything but a simple meal, or those particularly in the Middle East, who make war and violence a part of their lives. But we simply brood over the grimness of this whole situation, and then dismiss it as one heated propaganda!

Thus, we can conclude that the world is a place having all of it, the good, and the bad. Sum it, and the result is nil. That is, technological innovations that poster themselves as the road of progress for humanity, stand absolutely meaningless, if they cannot change the condition of humanity. They may make us march ahead of time, into the endless oceans of possibilities, but the true progress lies in improving the quality of man’s humanity to man.

The Earth’s Cry in the aftermath of another Big Bang

How vividly those memories of pretty smiles have etched my sands and clouds, for they are all I can hold onto now. As I’m leaning peacefully against Sun’s warm shoulder, my eyes flutter ecstatically from Venus’ bright, iridescent face and Mars’ ruby aurora, to Jupiter, Uranus, an excited Neptune in his sea-blue warrior drape, and Saturn, jumping in their little warrior games, to Mercury, SPINNING AGILELY THROUGH THIS PLAYFUL CIRCLE OF LIFE. And when my eyes shyly meet Sun’s, they know in one twinkling that the pleasure is shared. Indeed a blissful abode, this tender and placid wreath of love and warmth that had bound us together over these few billion years and nursed our little clan with benevolence.

Until…

Swirling and spinning in the great ocean of ether, we started to break away, its furious motion threw us hurtling in fiery rings. The unbearable pain of separation melted our hearts and made us sick.

YES, IT HURT! IT HURT! My surface blazed with tremendous volcanic eruptions. It always felt like something was gnawing and hitting against me, with an unsurpassable force. I throbbed and burned under the pangs of rift. When it was too much to take, I bled tears. There were the silent sobs, occasioned with the howling whimpers, all lamenting THE CRY OF THE GREAT VOID, until my oceans overflowed.

Then it was silent, FOR A WHILE. I remembered to remember what I should have. In death, there was peace. In despondence, there was calm. In the great void, there were condensed memories that helped me sail through, FOR IN DEATH, THERE WAS LIFE.

Unspoken Bond – An ode to my mother

They say a daughter is a mother’s second chance to live her life. They say that every girl dreams of finding a best friend in her mother. I am indeed very lucky to have the latter true. I hope that someday, I may give you the former.

When your arms cradle around me,

I become so small, a baby to lie

In the thick warmth of your angelic wings.

I just drown in the ocean of your love

However hard I try.

The ocean so pure, so sweet

That tells me to see the part that’s neat

Even in those who trick and cheat

And in this glorifying process,

I become a better human being.

You know when to show me the path of light.

And you know when to let me choose my own wrong and right

You are my sun, you are my moon.

You are my silk cocoon.

You are that selfless spirit, a luminous candle.

You impart me the courage to face my life’s tangles.

You give me the motivation to keep up the fight.

And when I fall, you tuck me up tight.

You are my solace, the serenity of the sky.

You are the cherry on the vanilla scoop,

The chocolate syrup upon my pie.

You are the anchor that helps me climb my mountains.

You are the beauty and magic of fountains.

You face the toughest battle of life with no complaints.

Just pure determination

And tomorrow you wake up ready to fight again.

Thank you for everything you have given me in this world.

Thank you for your love that’s like the sunshine unfurled.

Punjabiyat

Punjabi culture, Punjabi style, Punjabi food, Punjabi art, Punjabi language, Punjabi literature, Punjabi dance, Punjabi music, Punjabi festivities, Punjabi people, Punjabi hearts, Punjabi minds, Punjabi warriors, Punjabi freedom…..

Balle Balle

Chak de phatte

I’m sure you all have much, much more to add on to what I have just said. Yes, Punjab is a place so extraordinary, and its heritage so resplendently rich, that its picturesque wheat fields, its placid rivers, its ultimately fascinating bhangra and gidha, its enticingly finger-licking-good food, and its aesthetic hospitality together can provide the most amazingly unforgettable and wholesome experience. But for those of you who are not much pleased and convicted of its richness beyond its ‘balle balle’ and ‘chak de phatte’, I’m here to bewitch you for the next 5 minutes and to talk to you about Punjab’s breathtaking Punjabiness.

Punjab, the richest state in India that throbs with the vibrant culture of equally vibrant people, has always moved on the path of prosperity despite all odds. A state where dreams of moving with the times have blossomed among the lush green fields and fertile soil.

While Punjab has been the childhood playground of brave freedom fighters like Bhagat Singh, or gallant kings like Maharaja Ranjit Singh, Punjabi community is known for its openness, liberty and magnanimity. If Punjab is the land where colours fly so perfectly through their traditional festivities and celebrations, then Punjab is also the place where the holiest of Sikh shrines, Golden temple and sacred Hindu scriptures, the Vedas, have diversified. Punjab is that gifted land, where the oldest, Indus-valley civilization has spanned, and Punjab is that land where precious art and architecture has flourished and come through. And how can I not mention, the exuberance-filled bhangra and lively gidha dances? But friends, there’s more to it. Punjab is the home of some of the world’s oldest, grooviest musical instruments, dynamic poetry, ballads, epics and romances. But the heart of Punjab really lies in that simple mutiyar, decorated in bright hues and golden tikka, preparing lassi, or those little kids who are more than just proud to be born here, and in that simple kisaan who toils tirelessly to make sure that Punjab remains the Granary basket of India. The heart of Punjab lies in its people and their Punjabiness.

So yeah, certainly, Punjab rocks! Punjabiness rocks!
